Metro's retail footprint
Competitive position

Metro by T-Mobile is Boost's primary direct competitor in this zip — and it holds a commanding 5-to-1 retail presence advantage. In the prepaid segment, physical store proximity is a key purchase driver: unbanked and cash-paying customers transact in person, and switching cost is low when a competitor's store is closer. Metro's stores are clustered along the highest-traffic corridors (S Dixie Hwy, SW 184th St, SW 152nd St), while Boost's single location sits near the western edge of the zip on Quail Roost Drive — isolated from the Dixie Hwy retail spine where most prepaid activity is concentrated.

Brand strengths to leverage

Boost's $25/month bring-your-own-phone plan undercuts Metro's entry pricing and is a strong hook in cost-sensitive markets like 33157. As a hybrid MNO running on AT&T's towers (following the $23B spectrum sale), Boost now offers network quality comparable to Metro. The brand added 212,000 net subscribers in Q2 2025 nationally — early evidence the pricing narrative is gaining traction. In a high-remittance, cash-active community, Boost's positioning as a value-first no-contract carrier is well-matched to this zip's demographic profile.

Recommended store expansion locations
1
S Dixie Hwy corridor (Cutler Bay)
The stretch between 186th–192nd St on S Dixie Hwy has 3 Metro locations, 3 Western Union agents, a Dollar General, and a Dollar Tree — but no Boost. This is the highest co-traffic density node in the zip and the most direct competitive gap to close.
2
SW 152nd St node (northern zip)
Metro has a location at 11271 SW 152nd St alongside a Family Dollar and MoneyGram — a proven value-consumer cluster. A Boost store in this northern corridor would intercept customers before they reach Metro and capture the Palmetto Bay / Richmond Heights traffic.
3
SW 160th St / 97th Ave area
Metro's 9690 SW 160th St store sits adjacent to a Family Dollar and Quick Cash Auto Loans. This intersection sees consistent foot traffic from the value-consumer segment. A nearby Boost presence would create direct head-to-head competition at the store level.
4
Authorized dealer in money services hubs
High-volume check cashing locations (Check Cashing USA at SW 184th, Fast Payday Loans on S Dixie) draw exactly Boost's core customer: cash-transacting, unbanked, value-sensitive. Adding a Boost authorized dealer or co-located kiosk inside these venues could drive low-capex subscriber acquisition at proven traffic points.
